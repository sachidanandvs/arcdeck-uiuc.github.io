Slide Deck Human Evaluation 😊

This study compares the quality of slide decks automatically generated from research papers,
focusing on content and narrative flow.

Evaluation Criteria

For each paper, rank the three slide decks (A, B, C) on each of the five criteria below.

Narrative Flow

  1. Logical Progression — Does it follow a coherent structure (problem → method → evidence) without front-loading technical details?
  2. Cross-Slide Coherence — Do slides explicitly build on earlier ones, with clear logical transitions?
  3. Global Coherence — Do all slides support one unified narrative of explaining a paper?

Content

  1. Technical Depth — Does it include concrete technical substance (method details, experimental validation), rather than a few bullet-point summaries?

Visuals

  1. Visuals — Does the deck use figures, color theme, emphasis (bold/highlighting) effectively to make the presentation visually engaging and easy to follow?

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Select the topic area you are most confident evaluating.
  2. Each topic contains 5 research documents.
  3. For each document, review three anonymized slide decks (A, B, C) generated by different systems.
  4. Click the red Expand button below each slide deck to view it in full size for a clearer evaluation.
  5. Rank them from 1 (Best) to 3 (Worst) based on the criteria above.
  6. If needed click the link near paper title to open the reference paper the slide is based on.

Systems are anonymized, and A/B/C order may vary across documents.
Estimated completion time: 15–20 minutes 🙂
